About

Hi — I’m Chamu, and I really enjoy using mathematics to understand the natural world.

My interest primarily lies in biological systems, where I explore them using mathematical modelling and computational methods — particularly in areas like tumour growth, pattern formation, and nonlinear dynamics.

I created this site as a place to compile the projects I’ve worked on — from mathematical biology to more experimental ideas in computing and design. Along the way, I’ve also included a few side projects built purely out of curiosity and fun.

It’s become my little lab of ideas — part notebook, part gallery, and part playground for experiments in maths, computing, and design.

This space will keep evolving as I explore new directions, but at its heart it’s meant to be a small collection of things I’ve learned, built, and enjoyed working on.
